# Scenario Rubrics and O*NET Skills Reference

This document provides detailed rubrics and O*NET skill mappings for each scenario type in the assessment.

## Nursing Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Patient Care - Deteriorating Patient (Mrs. Johnson)

**O*NET Skills Assessed:**
- **Active Listening** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Understanding patient and family concerns
- **Critical Thinking** (Importance: 4.8/5) - Analyzing patient condition and making clinical decisions
- **Judgment and Decision Making** (Importance: 4.7/5) - Determining appropriate interventions
- **Monitoring** (Importance: 4.9/5) - Assessing vital signs and patient status
- **Reading Comprehension** (Importance: 4.2/5) - Understanding standing orders and protocols
- **Speaking** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Communicating with family and healthcare team
- **Writing** (Importance: 4.3/5) - Documenting assessments and interventions
- **Complex Problem Solving** (Importance: 4.6/5) - Managing multiple concerns simultaneously
- **Social Perceptiveness** (Importance: 4.4/5) - Understanding family distress and patient anxiety

**Detailed Rubric:**

| Level | Score | Criteria | Indicators |
|-------|-------|----------|------------|
| **Advanced** | 4 | Demonstrates comprehensive assessment, evidence-based interventions, proactive team coordination, and excellent communication | • Immediate comprehensive assessment (respiratory, cardiac, neurological)<br>• Evidence-based interventions (oxygen titration, positioning, medication administration per standing orders)<br>• Rapid team coordination (notify respiratory, charge nurse, rapid response if needed)<br>• Clear, empathetic family communication with regular updates<br>• Comprehensive documentation of all interventions and outcomes<br>• Recognition of potential complications and preventive measures |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Shows thorough assessment and appropriate interventions within scope | • Thorough assessment of key systems<br>• Appropriate interventions within nursing scope (oxygen adjustment, positioning)<br>• Documentation of findings and interventions<br>• Basic team communication (notify charge nurse, physician)<br>• Basic family communication |
| **Developing** | 2 | Demonstrates basic monitoring and documentation | • Basic monitoring of vital signs<br>• Documentation of changes observed<br>• Informing supervisor or charge nurse<br>• Limited intervention without comprehensive assessment |
| **Needs Development** | 1 | Shows minimal action or waits for direction | • Waiting for physician availability<br>• Minimal or no immediate intervention<br>• Lack of comprehensive assessment<br>• Poor or no communication with team/family |

### Scenario 2: Nurse Handoff - Shift Transition

**O*NET Skills Assessed:**
- **Active Listening** (Importance: 4.8/5) - Receiving and processing handoff information
- **Critical Thinking** (Importance: 4.7/5) - Analyzing handoff for gaps and concerns
- **Reading Comprehension** (Importance: 4.6/5) - Reviewing charts, labs, and documentation
- **Speaking** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Asking clarifying questions during handoff
- **Judgment and Decision Making** (Importance: 4.9/5) - Prioritizing patient care based on acuity
- **Time Management** (Importance: 4.4/5) - Managing time constraints effectively
- **Complex Problem Solving** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Identifying and addressing handoff gaps
- **Social Perceptiveness** (Importance: 4.2/5) - Recognizing urgency in rushed situations

**Detailed Rubric:**

| Level | Score | Criteria | Indicators |
|-------|-------|----------|------------|
| **Advanced** | 4 | Comprehensive handoff review with immediate prioritization and proactive safety measures | • Thorough review of all handoff information<br>• Immediate identification of concerns (e.g., elevated troponin, missing vitals)<br>• Asking clarifying questions about unclear information<br>• Rapid prioritization based on patient acuity<br>• Immediate assessment of high-risk patients<br>• Communication with oncoming team about concerns<br>• Comprehensive documentation of findings and actions<br>• Follow-up on missed information (e.g., addressing elevated troponin) |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Good handoff review with identification of concerns and prioritization | • Reviewing handoff information thoroughly<br>• Identifying key concerns and asking clarifying questions<br>• Prioritizing patient care appropriately<br>• Conducting focused assessments on identified concerns |
| **Developing** | 2 | Basic acceptance of handoff with minimal questioning | • Basic acceptance of handoff information<br>• Proceeding with routine care<br>• Limited questioning or clarification<br>• May miss important concerns |
| **Needs Development** | 1 | Passive acceptance without critical review | • Accepting handoff without questioning<br>• No identification of concerns or gaps<br>• Missing critical information (e.g., elevated troponin)<br>• No prioritization of care |

### Scenario 3: Patient Case Management - Complex Patient (Mrs. Martinez)

**O*NET Skills Assessed:**
- **Critical Thinking** (Importance: 4.9/5) - Analyzing complex patient presentation
- **Judgment and Decision Making** (Importance: 4.8/5) - Making clinical decisions about interventions
- **Monitoring** (Importance: 4.7/5) - Ongoing assessment of patient condition
- **Complex Problem Solving** (Importance: 4.9/5) - Managing multiple comorbidities and complications
- **Active Listening** (Importance: 4.4/5) - Understanding family concerns
- **Speaking** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Communicating with interdisciplinary team and family
- **Reading Comprehension** (Importance: 4.6/5) - Reviewing medical history and current orders
- **Writing** (Importance: 4.4/5) - Documenting comprehensive assessments
- **Social Perceptiveness** (Importance: 4.3/5) - Recognizing patient and family distress

**Detailed Rubric:**

| Level | Score | Criteria | Indicators |
|-------|-------|----------|------------|
| **Advanced** | 4 | Comprehensive assessment with recognition of complications and proactive intervention | • Comprehensive assessment of all systems (neurological, cardiovascular, renal, metabolic)<br>• Recognition of potential complications (delirium, worsening UTI, dehydration, medication timing issues)<br>• Immediate evidence-based interventions (check antibiotic timing, assess for delirium, monitor hydration status)<br>• Coordination with interdisciplinary team (physician, pharmacy, case management, physical therapy)<br>• Clear, proactive communication with family addressing concerns<br>• Patient advocacy for timely interventions<br>• Comprehensive documentation<br>• Development of care plan addressing all concerns |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Thorough assessment with identification of key concerns and appropriate interventions | • Thorough assessment of key systems<br>• Identification of main concerns (confusion, dehydration, medication timing)<br>• Appropriate interventions within scope<br>• Communication with healthcare team<br>• Basic family communication |

| **Needs Development** | 1 | Minimal assessment or passive approach | • Minimal assessment<br>• Waiting for physician to round<br>• No proactive intervention<br>• Poor recognition of complications |

- **Active Listening** (Importance: 4.7/5) - Understanding patient and family concerns
- **Speaking** (Importance: 4.8/5) - Educating patient and family about medications

## Education Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Instruction - Differentiating for Struggling Students

**O*NET Skills Assessed:**
- **Instructing** (Importance: 4.9/5) - Teaching and explaining concepts
- **Learning Strategies** (Importance: 4.7/5) - Selecting and using appropriate teaching methods
- **Active Listening** (Importance: 4.6/5) - Understanding student needs and confusion
- **Critical Thinking** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Analyzing learning gaps and adjusting instruction
- **Social Perceptiveness** (Importance: 4.4/5) - Recognizing student engagement and understanding
- **Speaking** (Importance: 4.8/5) - Explaining concepts clearly
- **Complex Problem Solving** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Addressing diverse learning needs simultaneously
- **Judgment and Decision Making** (Importance: 4.6/5) - Deciding when and how to adjust instruction

**Detailed Rubric:**

| Level | Score | Criteria | Indicators |
|-------|-------|----------|------------|
| **Advanced** | 4 | Immediate assessment of learning gaps with multiple strategies and comprehensive differentiation | • Immediate assessment of learning gaps using formative assessment<br>• Implementation of multiple teaching strategies (visual, kinesthetic, auditory)<br>• Differentiation for all learners (struggling and advanced)<br>• Use of formative assessment to check understanding<br>• Adjustment of instruction to ensure all students understand<br>• Engagement of all students in learning<br>• Reflection on effectiveness and further adjustments |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Good responsive teaching with differentiation | • Pausing to assess understanding<br>• Using alternative teaching strategies<br>• Differentiating instruction for struggling students<br>• Checking comprehension before proceeding |
| **Developing** | 2 | Basic awareness with limited differentiation | • Providing brief review<br>• Offering individual help after class<br>• Limited differentiation strategies<br>• Moving on without ensuring understanding |
| **Needs Development** | 1 | Rigid adherence to lesson plan | • Continuing with lesson plan regardless of student needs<br>• Offering help only after class<br>• No differentiation or adjustment<br>• Poor recognition of student struggles |

## General Technical/Professional Scenarios

**O*NET Skills Assessed:**
- **Critical Thinking** (Importance: 4.8/5) - Analyzing problems systematically
- **Complex Problem Solving** (Importance: 4.9/5) - Addressing complex technical issues
- **Systems Analysis** (Importance: 4.6/5) - Understanding system components and interactions
- **Judgment and Decision Making** (Importance: 4.7/5) - Making decisions about troubleshooting approach
- **Reading Comprehension** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Understanding logs, documentation, and error messages
- **Writing** (Importance: 4.4/5) - Documenting findings and solutions
- **Active Learning** (Importance: 4.3/5) - Learning from problems and solutions
- **Monitoring** (Importance: 4.5/5) - Observing system behavior and patterns

**Detailed Rubric:**

| Level | Score | Criteria | Indicators |
|-------|-------|----------|------------|
| **Advanced** | 4 | Systematic investigation with comprehensive data collection and preventive measures | • Comprehensive logging implementation<br>• Data pattern analysis<br>• Hypothesis formation and testing<br>• Implementation of fix with monitoring<br>• Creation of preventive measures<br>• Documentation of process and solution<br>• Knowledge sharing with team |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Methodical problem-solving approach | • Systematic data collection<br>• Pattern analysis<br>• Hypothesis formation and testing<br>• Documentation of findings<br>• Implementation of solution |
| **Developing** | 2 | Basic troubleshooting with limited systematic approach | • Trying common fixes<br>• Monitoring if problem persists<br>• Limited systematic analysis<br>• Basic documentation |
| **Needs Development** | 1 | Passive approach without independent problem-solving | • Reporting to supervisor and waiting<br>• No independent troubleshooting<br>• Limited analysis or investigation<br>• Poor documentation |

## How to Use These Rubrics

### For Evaluators:
1. Read the user's open-ended response carefully
2. Compare the response against the rubric indicators for each level
3. Assign the highest level where the response demonstrates most indicators
4. Provide specific feedback referencing the rubric criteria

### For Users:
1. Review the rubric criteria before responding
2. Aim to address multiple indicators from the "Advanced" level
3. Be specific about your actions, reasoning, and approach
4. Consider all aspects: assessment, intervention, communication, documentation

### Scoring Guidelines:
- **Level 4 (Advanced)**: Response demonstrates 80%+ of advanced indicators
- **Level 3 (Proficient)**: Response demonstrates 60%+ of proficient indicators
- **Level 2 (Developing)**: Response demonstrates 40%+ of developing indicators
- **Level 1 (Needs Development)**: Response demonstrates minimal indicators or shows inappropriate approach

## O*NET Skill Importance Scale

Skills are rated on a 0-5 scale where:
- **5.0**: Extremely Important
- **4.0-4.9**: Very Important
- **3.0-3.9**: Important
- **2.0-2.9**: Somewhat Important
- **1.0-1.9**: Not Important

These importance ratings come from O*NET's occupational data and reflect how critical each skill is for successful job performance in the assessed role.
